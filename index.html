Visual Aid Prototype Site

Hello, welcome to our prototype website for making lectures more accessible to those with vision impairments.

With this site you can upload your lecture to the site and our agent will join you in watching and identify what images, graphs, charts and other visual aids are present in the lecture, what they mean in the context of the lecture, and generate descriptions live to communicate this to you.

How to Use This Site

Uploading your lecture

To upload your lecture, there is an input box, that asks for a URL link. Simply post the link to your live talk in the URL input box labeled "Lecture link", and press enter or click the button "Submit lecture and contuine", to have the aid be able to access the lecture. After submitting you will be redirected to the description page, where the tool will provide descriptions on visual information as it is detected. (NOTE: As this is a prototype, this is not fully functional yet and will only work for the links listed under Mock Scenarios)

Generating and Speaking Descripton

After connecting to lecture, the aid will generate descriptions as it detects they are necessary, playing a "Ding" audio cue for each new generated description. The most recent description can be read by clicking the "Play Audio Description" button. The description block will be updated as more descriptions are generated. The user will be able to go back and have previous description be read out loud by clicking the "Previous Description" and "Next Description" buttons.

How to use simulated examples

Included in this prototype to help demonstrate our hopes for this webiste are mock scenarios that showcase: how the site will notify a user of descriptions and what the content of the descriptions will be.

Each scenario will have an audio recording of a short presentation that describes figures and images not shown or described. When the speaker indicates that a visual is being described, an audio cue will play and a description will be played. To try out the simulated examples yourself copy one of the listed links into the URL input box!

Note: Many features are not operational, as this site is just a prototype
